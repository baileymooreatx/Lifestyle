# Processed Meats

Processed meats are classified as **Group 1 carcinogens** ("carcinogenic to
humans") by the World Health Organization's International Agency for Research on
Cancer (IARC) because sufficient evidence shows they cause cancer, particularly
**colorectal cancer**. This classification reflects the *strength of evidence*,
not the *magnitude of risk* (which is lower than tobacco, though both are Group
1).

## Definition

Processed meats are defined by the World Health Organization (WHO) and the
International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) as meat that has been 
**transformed through salting, curing, fermentation, smoking, or other
processes** to enhance flavor or improve preservation.

Crucially, this definition includes meat subjected to chemical preservation
(such as adding nitrates or nitrites) but **excludes** fresh meat that has only
undergone simple mechanical processes like cutting, grinding, or mixing. While
most processed meats are made from pork or beef, the category also includes
products made from poultry, offal (e.g., liver), or meat by-products like 
blood.  

Common examples include:

* **Cured/Smoked meats:** Bacon, ham (including prosciutto and serrano),
  sausages, hot dogs, salami, pepperoni, chorizo, and pastrami.
* **Preserved/Dried meats:** Beef jerky, biltong, and corned beef.
* **Deli/Luncheon meats:** Sliced cold cuts like bologna, roast beef (deli
  style), and turkey ham.
* **Canned meats:** Spam, canned corned beef, and meat-based preparations or
  sauces containing preserved meat.

## Key Carcinogenic Mechanisms

### N-Nitroso Compounds (NOCs)

Processed meats contain **nitrates and nitrites** added as preservatives. In the
acidic environment of the stomach, these react with amines from meat proteins to
form **N-nitrosamines**, potent carcinogens that damage DNA in colon and stomach
cells. Heme iron (abundant in red meat) accelerates this process.

### Cooking-Generated Carcinogens

High-temperature cooking (grilling, frying, smoking) produces:

- **Heterocyclic amines (HCAs)**: Formed when amino acids and creatine react at
  high heat; mutagenic to colon cells.
- **Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs)**: Created when fat drips onto
  flames during smoking/grilling, depositing on meat; known to cause DNA
  mutations.

### Additional Contributing Factors

- **Heme iron**: Promotes oxidative stress and lipid peroxidation, damaging
  colon lining.
- **High salt content**: Damages stomach lining, increasing gastric cancer risk.
- **Gut microbiota disruption**: Processed meats may promote harmful bacteria
  that produce inflammatory metabolites linked to tumor growth.

## Risk Magnitude

Consuming **50 grams daily** (~1 hot dog or 4 bacon strips) increases colorectal
cancer risk by **18%**. While lower than smoking's risk, the evidence for
causation is equally robust. No safe threshold has been established; risk
rises with consumption.
