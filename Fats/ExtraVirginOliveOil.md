# Extra Virgin Olive Oil (EVOO)  

Extra virgin olive oil is the least processed form of olive oil, retaining 
the highest concentration of nutrients, antioxidants, and healthy fats. It is a 
cornerstone of the Mediterranean diet and offers extensive health benefits 
supported by scientific research.  

## Nutritional Profile  

One tablespoon (approximately 14 grams) of Extra Virgin Olive Oil typically
contains:

* **Calories**: ~119–126
* **Total Fat**: 14 grams
    * **Monounsaturated Fat (MUFA)**: ~9.8–10 grams (primarily **oleic acid**,
      an omega-9 fatty acid)
    * **Polyunsaturated Fat**: ~1.4 grams
    * **Saturated Fat**: ~1.9 grams
* **Vitamins**:
    * **Vitamin E**: ~10–19% of the Daily Value (DV)
    * **Vitamin K**: ~3–10% of the DV

Unlike refined oils, EVOO is rich in **polyphenols** (such as oleocanthal,
hydroxytyrosol, and oleuropein) and other bioactive compounds that act as
powerful antioxidants.

## Heart Health Benefits

EVOO is strongly linked to cardiovascular health through several mechanisms:

* **Cholesterol Management**: The high monounsaturated fat content helps lower
  LDL ("bad") cholesterol while maintaining or increasing HDL ("good")
  cholesterol.
* **Blood Pressure**: Regular consumption is associated with lowered blood
  pressure.
* **Clot Prevention**: Polyphenols in EVOO can prevent blood platelets from
  clumping together, reducing the risk of heart attacks and strokes.
* **Oxidative Protection**: Antioxidants protect LDL cholesterol from oxidation,
  a key step in the development of heart disease.

Studies, such as the PREDIMED trial, indicate that consuming a Mediterranean
diet rich in EVOO can reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease, heart attack,
and stroke by approximately 30% compared to low-fat diets.

## Anti-Inflammatory and Antioxidant Properties

Chronic inflammation is a driver of many diseases, including cancer, heart
disease, and Alzheimer's.

* **Oleocanthal**: This specific polyphenol in EVOO has anti-inflammatory
  effects comparable to ibuprofen. It provides the characteristic peppery
  sensation in the throat when tasting high-quality oil.
* **Oxidative Stress**: The abundant antioxidants (vitamin E and polyphenols)
  combat oxidative stress, which damages DNA, proteins, and lipids in the body.

## Disease Prevention

Research suggests EVOO may help prevent or manage several serious conditions.

* **Cancer**: Components like oleic acid, hydroxytyrosol, and squalene have
  demonstrated anti-cancer properties. Studies link higher olive oil consumption
  to reduced risks of breast and colorectal cancers.
* **Type 2 Diabetes**: EVOO helps regulate blood sugar levels and improves
  insulin sensitivity. The polyphenol oleuropein stimulates insulin release,
  aiding in glycemic control.
* **Neurodegenerative Diseases**: Antioxidants in EVOO, particularly
  oleocanthal, may help clear beta-amyloid plaques in the brain, potentially
  slowing the progression of Alzheimer's disease and protecting against
  cognitive decline.

## Other Health Benefits

* **Bone Health**: Higher intake of olive oil is associated with increased bone
  density and a lower risk of osteoporosis, likely due to its anti-inflammatory
  effects.
* **Gut Health**: EVOO promotes a healthy gut microbiome by fostering the growth
  of beneficial bacteria like *Bifidobacteria*.
* **Weight Management**: Despite being calorie-dense, diets rich in EVOO are
  linked to better weight management and reduced body fat compared to low-fat
  diets, possibly due to increased satiety and metabolic benefits.
* **Mental Health**: Emerging research suggests that the nutrients in EVOO
  support the central nervous system and may help alleviate symptoms of
  depression.
* **Nutrient Absorption**: The healthy fats in EVOO enhance the absorption of
  fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, and K) from other foods.

## Usage and Storage

To maximize benefits:

* **Cooking**: EVOO has a smoke point between 350°F and 410°F (175°C–210°C),
  making it safe for sautéing, roasting, and frying without significant nutrient
  loss.
* **Storage**: Store in a cool, dark place to prevent oxidation. Nutritional
  quality, particularly polyphenol content, diminishes over time; it is best
  consumed within a year of harvest.
* **Dosage**: Benefits are observed with moderate consumption, such as 1.5 to 4
  tablespoons per day, ideally replacing less healthy fats like butter or
  margarine.
