# Oranges

Oranges are a **nutritional powerhouse**, offering a dense profile
of vitamins, minerals, fiber, and antioxidants that support immune function,
heart health, and disease prevention.

## Key Nutrients

A single medium orange provides a significant portion of daily nutritional
requirements with low caloric impact. One navel orange typically contains
approximately **73 calories**, **16.5g of carbohydrates**, **2.8g of fiber**,
and **1.3g of protein**, with negligible fat.

The most notable nutrient is **vitamin C**, with one large orange providing over
**100% of the daily value** (approximately 83–116% depending on size and
variety). This water-soluble antioxidant is crucial for preventing cell damage,
forming collagen, and boosting the immune system. Oranges are also an excellent
source of **folate** (vitamin B9), essential for DNA synthesis and cell
division, making them particularly important for pregnant women to prevent birth
defects. Additionally, they provide **potassium**, which regulates heartbeat and
blood pressure, and smaller amounts of **calcium**, **thiamine**, and **vitamin
A** precursors like beta-cryptoxanthin.

## Health Benefits

### Immune System and Disease Prevention

The high concentration of **vitamin C** and **folate** strengthens the immune
system, helping the body resist viruses and bacteria. Oranges contain 
**flavonoids** like hesperidin and narirutin, which possess anti-inflammatory,
antiviral, and antimicrobial properties. These compounds, along with other
antioxidants, help neutralize free radicals, potentially reducing the risk of
chronic diseases including **cancer**. Studies suggest that flavonoids in orange
peels may inhibit cancer cell growth and prevent the formation of new blood
vessels that tumors need.

### Heart and Circulatory Health

Regular consumption supports cardiovascular health by helping to **lower blood
pressure** and **reduce cholesterol levels**. The potassium content aids in
blood circulation, while fiber helps manage cholesterol. Research indicates that
the antioxidant hesperidin may improve blood vessel health and reduce oxidative
damage. Furthermore, citrus consumption has been linked to a reduced risk of *
*stroke**, with one study showing a 19% risk reduction in women who consumed
higher amounts of citrus fruits.

### Digestion and Blood Sugar Control

With approximately **3 grams of fiber** per fruit, oranges promote healthy
digestion and relieve constipation. This fiber also slows sugar absorption,
preventing blood sugar spikes and making oranges a suitable choice for managing
**diabetes**. The fructose in the flesh, combined with fiber, helps regulate
blood glucose levels effectively.

### Skin, Eye, and Bone Health

**Vitamin C** is vital for **collagen production**, which maintains skin
elasticity, reduces wrinkles, and protects against UV damage. For eye health,
nutrients in oranges may slow the progression of **age-related macular
degeneration** and cataracts. The fruit also contributes to bone strength,
providing about **6% of the daily calcium requirement** alongside other
bone-supporting minerals.

### Kidney Stone Prevention

Oranges are a good source of **citrates**, which help inhibit the formation of
kidney stones. Potassium also plays a role in facilitating the elimination of
stones from the body, reducing the risk of recurrence.

## Potential Risks

While generally safe, excessive consumption can lead to adverse effects due to
high **vitamin C** intake, such as nausea, diarrhea, abdominal cramps, or
headaches. In rare cases, very high intake may increase the risk of **kidney
stones** in susceptible individuals. The acidity can aggravate **heartburn** or
acid reflux in some people. Additionally, individuals taking certain **blood
pressure medications** should exercise caution, as the potassium in oranges may
interact with these drugs. Those with citrus allergies should also avoid them.
