## Stoic daily routine  
Nothing extreme, just a few habits that build focus, calm, and resilience over time.

### Morning (5–10 minutes)

#### 1. Set your mindset
Inspired by Marcus Aurelius

Ask yourself:
* *What might go wrong today?*
* *How will I respond calmly and rationally?*

👉 You’re not being negative—you’re preparing.

#### 2. Choose your focus
Pick 1–3 things you **can control** today:
* Your effort at work
* Your attitude
* Your behavior toward others

👉 This aligns with the teachings of Epictetus

### During the day

#### 3. Pause before reacting
When something annoying happens:
* Take a breath
* Ask: *“Is this in my control?”*
  * If no → let it go
  * If yes → act calmly and deliberately

#### 4. Practice restraint
From Seneca’s teachings:
* Don’t argue just to win
* Don’t complain unnecessarily
* Speak less, but more thoughtfully

#### 5. Optional: short reset
* Take a walk without your phone
* Clear your head

👉 Helps you reset your perspective

### Evening (5–10 minutes)

#### 6. Reflect on your day
Ask:
* What did I do well?
* Where did I overreact?
* What can I improve tomorrow?

👉 No guilt — just learning

#### 7. Perspective check (light version of *memento mori*)
* Remember life is finite
* Ask: *“Did I focus on what actually matters today?”*

### Summary  
If you only do 3 things:
1. **Morning:** Decide what you control
2. **Day:** Pause before reacting
3. **Night:** Reflect briefly

👉 That alone gets you most of the benefit

### What changes over time
If you stick with this, you’ll notice:
* Less emotional overreaction
* Better decision-making
* More consistent energy and focus
