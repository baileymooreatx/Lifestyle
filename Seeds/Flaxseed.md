# Flaxseeds

Flaxseeds are considered a **superfood** due to their dense concentration of
three primary bioactive components: **alpha-linolenic acid (ALA)**, **dietary
fiber**, and **lignans**. To maximize the absorption of these nutrients, experts
recommend consuming them in **ground form** rather than whole, as whole seeds
often pass through the digestive tract undigested.

## Heart Health and Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Flaxseeds are one of the richest plant-based sources of **omega-3 fatty acids**,
specifically ALA. Regular consumption has been linked to significant
cardiovascular improvements:

* **Cholesterol Management**: Studies indicate that flaxseed can lower total
  cholesterol and LDL ("bad") cholesterol while potentially increasing HDL ("
  good") cholesterol.
* **Blood Pressure Reduction**: Clinical trials have shown that consuming ground
  flaxseed daily can significantly reduce both systolic and diastolic blood
  pressure, particularly in individuals with hypertension.
* **Inflammation Reduction**: The anti-inflammatory properties of ALA help
  reduce arterial inflammation, lowering the risk of atherosclerosis and heart
  disease.

## Digestive Health and Fiber Content

Flaxseeds are composed of approximately **40% dietary fiber**, split between
soluble (25%) and insoluble (75%) types.

* **Regularity**: Insoluble fiber adds bulk to stool and accelerates food
  transit through the gut, effectively preventing constipation.
* **Blood Sugar Control**: Soluble fiber slows digestion and the absorption of
  carbohydrates, which helps regulate blood sugar levels and improve insulin
  sensitivity. This makes flaxseeds beneficial for managing type 2 diabetes and
  metabolic syndrome.
* **Satiety**: The high fiber content promotes a feeling of fullness, which can
  aid in weight management by reducing overall calorie intake.

## Cancer Prevention and Antioxidant Properties

Flaxseeds contain up to **800 times more lignans** than other plant foods.
Lignans act as both antioxidants and phytoestrogens.

* **Hormone-Related Cancers**: Due to their ability to modulate estrogen
  metabolism, lignans may help reduce the risk of hormone-sensitive cancers,
  such as breast and prostate cancer.
* **Cell Protection**: The antioxidant activity protects cells from oxidative
  stress and DNA damage, which are key factors in cancer development. Some
  research suggests lignans can enhance the effectiveness of certain cancer
  treatments.
  
## Skin Health and Additional Nutrients

Beyond the "big three" nutrients, flaxseeds offer a range of other health
supports:

* **Skin Condition**: The combination of essential fatty acids and vitamin E 
  (tocopherol) supports skin hydration, reduces dryness, and may accelerate  
  wound healing.  
* **Micronutrients**: They are a good source of B vitamins like **thiamine** 
  as well as essential minerals including 
  * **magnesium**  
  * **phosphorus** 
  * **copper** 
  * **manganese**  


## Recommended Intake and Usage

To reap these benefits, a daily intake of **1 to 2 tablespoons of ground
flaxseed** is generally recommended. Because the oils in flaxseeds can oxidize
and go rancid, ground flaxseed should be stored in an **airtight container in
the refrigerator or freezer**.
