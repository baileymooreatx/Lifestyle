# Hemp Hearts

Hemp hearts are the soft, inner part of hemp seeds after the hard outer shell
has been removed. Also known as hulled or shelled hemp seeds, they come from the
Cannabis sativa plant but contain negligible amounts of THC (less than 0.3%),
meaning **they will not produce a psychoactive effect**. They are characterized
by a **soft texture** and a **mild, nutty flavor**, making them easier to digest
and more versatile in cooking than whole hemp seeds.

## Complete Protein Profile

Hemp hearts are a **complete plant-based protein**, containing all **nine
essential amino acids** that the human body cannot produce on its own. A
standard 3-tablespoon (30g) serving provides approximately **9.5 to 10 grams of
protein**, which is nearly double the amount found in similar servings of chia
or flax seeds.

Their protein digestibility-corrected amino acid score (PDCAA) ranges from **0.5
to 0.62**, making them one of the most digestible plant protein sources
available, though slightly lower than soy or animal products. This makes them an
**ideal protein source for vegetarians and vegans** looking to support muscle
repair and immune function.

## Essential Fatty Acids

One of the most distinct nutritional features of hemp hearts is their **ideal 3:
1 ratio of omega-6 to omega-3 fatty acids**. This balance is crucial for
reducing inflammation and supporting cardiovascular health.

A single serving contains nearly **15 grams of fat**, predominantly
polyunsaturated fats, including:

* **Alpha-linolenic acid (ALA)**: An essential omega-3 fatty acid linked to
  heart health.
* **Gamma-linolenic acid (GLA)**: An omega-6 fatty acid with anti-inflammatory
  properties that may help with skin conditions and hormonal balance.

Unlike animal sources of fat, hemp hearts are **cholesterol-free**.

## Key Vitamins and Minerals

Hemp hearts are nutrient-dense, providing significant percentages of daily
recommended values in a small serving:

* **Magnesium**: A 3-tablespoon serving offers about **210 mg**, roughly **50%
  of the daily requirement**. Magnesium is vital for blood sugar regulation,
  bone density, and muscle function.
* **Phosphorus**: Provides approximately **70% of the daily value**, essential
  for building strong bones and teeth.
* **Iron**: Contains about **13% of the daily value**, which is particularly
  beneficial for plant-based eaters who need to monitor non-heme iron intake.
* **Zinc**: Offers around **20% of the daily needs**, supporting immune system
  health.
* **Manganese**: A single serving can exceed **100% of the daily value**, aiding
  in metabolism and bone formation.
* **B Vitamins**: Rich in **Thiamine (B1)** and **vitamin E**, an antioxidant
  that protects cells from damage.

## Heart and Cardiovascular Health

The combination of **arginine** (an amino acid that converts to nitric oxide)
and healthy fats helps relax blood vessels, potentially lowering **blood
pressure** and reducing the risk of heart disease. The omega fatty acids also
help lower triglyceride levels and reduce plaque buildup in arteries.

## Digestive Health

While hulled hearts have less fiber than whole seeds, they still provide dietary
fiber that aids in **regularity and gut health**. The fiber content helps
prevent constipation and supports a healthy microbiome.

## Anti-inflammatory

The **GLA content** in hemp hearts acts as a natural anti-inflammatory agent.
This can help alleviate symptoms of **eczema, dry skin, and rheumatoid 
arthritis**. The balanced fatty acid profile supports skin hydration and barrier
function.

## Bone Strength

High levels of **magnesium and phosphorus** work synergistically to maintain
**bone mineral density**, reducing the risk of osteoporosis and fractures as you
age.

## How to Incorporate Them

Due to their soft texture and mild taste, hemp hearts require no grinding or
soaking and can be eaten raw. Common ways to consume them include:

* **Smoothies and Shakes**: Blended in for a protein boost without altering
  flavor.
* **Toppings**: Sprinkled over yogurt, oatmeal, cereal, or salads for added
  crunch and nutrition.
* **Baking**: Mixed into muffin, bread, or cookie batters.
* **Hemp Milk**: Blended with water and strained to create a dairy-free milk
  alternative.

## Storage

Because of their high fat content, hemp hearts can go rancid if exposed to heat
and light. It is best to store opened packages in the **refrigerator**, where
they can last up to a year.
