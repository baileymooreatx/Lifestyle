# Black Pepper

## Nutritional Profile

Black pepper is a nutrient-dense spice that provides significant amounts of
vitamins and minerals even in small serving sizes. One tablespoon (approximately
6.9 grams) of ground black pepper contains roughly **17 calories**, **4.4 grams
of carbohydrates**, and **2 grams of dietary fiber**. It is virtually fat-free
and cholesterol-free.

The spice is particularly rich in **manganese**, providing about 18% of the
Daily Value (DV) per tablespoon, which is essential for bone health, wound
healing, and metabolism. It is also a notable source of **vitamin K** (13% DV),
crucial for blood clotting and bone metabolism, and **iron** (10% DV), which
supports healthy red blood cell production. Other minerals present include
copper, calcium, magnesium, potassium, and zinc.

## Key Bioactive Compounds

The primary health benefits of black pepper are attributed to **piperine**, a
bioactive alkaloid that accounts for 2–9% of the spice's weight. Piperine is
responsible for the pungent taste and possesses potent **antioxidant** and 
**anti-inflammatory** properties. It helps reduce free radical damage to cells
and inhibits inflammatory pathways linked to chronic conditions like arthritis
and heart disease.

Black pepper also contains volatile oils, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds
that contribute to its antibacterial and therapeutic effects. These compounds
work synergistically to support overall cellular health and protect against
oxidative stress.

## Enhanced Nutrient Absorption

One of the most distinct nutritional benefits of black pepper is its ability to
significantly **increase the bioavailability** of other nutrients. Piperine
enhances the absorption of vitamins and minerals such as **vitamin C**, 
**selenium**, **beta-carotene**, and **B-complex vitamins**.

This effect is most famously observed when black pepper is consumed with 
**turmeric**; piperine can boost the absorption of curcumin (the active compound
in turmeric) by up to **2,000%**. By improving nutrient uptake, black pepper
helps the body utilize food and supplements more effectively, maximizing the
nutritional value of meals.

## Digestive and Metabolic Support

Black pepper stimulates the secretion of **hydrochloric acid** in the stomach,
which is essential for the proper digestion of proteins and the absorption of
nutrients. Its carminative properties help reduce intestinal gas and discomfort.

Additionally, black pepper exhibits **thermogenic** properties, meaning it can
slightly increase body temperature and metabolic rate. This process may aid in
fat burning and weight management. The fiber content also contributes to
digestive regularity and prolonged satiety.
