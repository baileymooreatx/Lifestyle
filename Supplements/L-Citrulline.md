# L-Citrulline  

L-citrulline is a non-essential amino acid that serves as a precursor to
L-arginine and significantly boosts **nitric oxide (NO)** production. Unlike
direct L-arginine supplementation, L-citrulline is absorbed more efficiently by
the body, bypassing liver metabolism to reliably increase plasma arginine
levels. Its primary benefits center on vascular health, exercise performance, 
and metabolic function.  

## Cardiovascular Health and Blood Pressure  

The most well-documented benefit of L-citrulline is its ability to improve 
**endothelial function** and lower blood pressure. By increasing nitric oxide
synthesis, it promotes **vasodilation** (widening of blood vessels), which
reduces arterial stiffness and improves blood flow.  

* **Blood Pressure Reduction:** Clinical studies indicate that daily doses of 
  **6 grams** can significantly lower both systolic and diastolic blood pressure
  in individuals with pre-hypertension and hypertension. It is particularly
  effective at reducing blood pressure reactivity during stress, such as cold
  exposure or isometric exercise.  
* **Arterial Stiffness:** Chronic supplementation (4–8 weeks) has been shown to
  reduce pulse wave velocity and the augmentation index, markers of arterial
  stiffness, especially in older adults and post-menopausal women.  
* **Heart Health:** Improved vascular resilience may help protect against
  endothelial dysfunction and support overall cardiac function, potentially
  aiding in conditions like heart failure.  

## Exercise Performance and Recovery

L-citrulline is widely used in sports nutrition to enhance physical performance
and accelerate recovery, though its efficacy depends on the type of exercise and
dosing strategy.

* **Endurance and Power:** Supplementation (typically **6 to 8 grams** of
  citrulline malate or **6 grams** of pure L-citrulline) has been shown to
  improve time-trial performance in cycling, increase power output, and enhance
  oxygen uptake kinetics during high-intensity exercise.
* **Fatigue Reduction:** It aids in the removal of metabolic waste products like
  **ammonia** and **lactic acid** via the urea cycle, which can delay muscle
  fatigue and reduce perceived exertion.
* **Recovery:** Regular use may decrease muscle soreness (DOMS) and improve
  recovery times between sessions. Note that **chronic supplementation** (7+
  days) is generally more effective for endurance than single acute doses,
  although single doses of citrulline malate are commonly used pre-workout.

## Erectile Dysfunction and Sexual Health

Due to its vasodilatory effects, L-citrulline is used as a natural intervention
for mild to moderate **erectile dysfunction (ED)**.

* **Mechanism:** By improving blood flow to the penile tissue through nitric
  oxide production, it can help achieve and maintain erections.
* **Efficacy:** While studies suggest it is **less potent** than pharmaceutical
  PDE5 inhibitors (e.g., Viagra), it is considered a safe alternative for men
  with mild ED. A typical effective dose is around **1.5 to 3 grams daily**,
  with improvements often observed after one month of consistent use.

## Metabolic and Immune Support

Emerging research highlights broader systemic benefits beyond circulation:

* **Metabolic Health:** Supplementation may improve glucose homeostasis, reduce
  insulin resistance, and lower inflammatory markers (such as TNF-α) in
  individuals with type 2 diabetes or obesity.
* **Immune Function:** L-citrulline plays a role in T-cell metabolism and may
  help modulate immune responses, potentially reducing chronic low-grade
  inflammation associated with aging ("inflamm-aging").
* **Muscle Preservation:** It stimulates muscle protein synthesis, which can
  help prevent sarcopenia (muscle loss) in older adults and support muscle
  maintenance during caloric restriction or illness.

## Recommended Dosage and Forms

The optimal dosage varies based on the intended health outcome. L-citrulline is
available primarily as **pure L-citrulline** or **citrulline malate** (bound to
malic acid).

| Goal                               | Recommended Form  | Daily Dosage                   | Duration          |
|:-----------------------------------|:------------------|:-------------------------------|:------------------|
| **General Cardiovascular Support** | Pure L-citrulline | 3–6 g (divided)                | Ongoing           |
| **Blood Pressure Management**      | Pure L-citrulline | 5–6 g (divided)                | 4–8+ weeks        |
| **Exercise Performance**           | Pure L-citrulline | 6 g/day                        | 7+ days (chronic) |
| **Pre-Workout (Acute)**            | Citrulline Malate | 8 g (approx. 4.5 g citrulline) | 30–60 min before  |
| **Mild Erectile Dysfunction**      | Pure L-citrulline | 1.5–3 g (divided)              | 1+ month          |

*Note: Pure L-citrulline is often preferred for cardiovascular and ED benefits,
while citrulline malate is popular in pre-workout contexts, though evidence for
single-dose efficacy is weaker compared to chronic loading.*

### Dosage by Goal

### Exercise Performance & Muscle Pumps

* **Pure L-Citrulline:** Take **6–8 grams** approximately 30–60 minutes before
  training.
* **Citrulline Malate:** Take **8 to 10 grams** (assuming a 2:1 ratio) 30–60
  minutes pre-workout.  
  *Note: This equates to roughly 5.3 to 6.7 grams of actual citrulline. Many
  commercial pre-workouts under-dose this ingredient (listing 6g of malate
  which yields only ~4g of citrulline), so checking the label ratio is
  critical.*
* **Chronic Loading:** For endurance benefits (e.g., cycling time trials), a
  daily dose of **6 grams** taken consistently for **7+ days** prior to an event
  is more effective than a single acute dose.

### Blood Pressure & Cardiovascular Health

* **Dosage:** **3 to 6 grams** of pure L-citrulline daily.
* **Protocol:** Split into divided doses (e.g., 2 grams, three times daily) to
  maintain stable plasma arginine levels.
* **Duration:** Benefits typically require **4to 8 weeks** of consistent daily
  use. Single doses do not significantly lower blood pressure.

### Erectile Dysfunction (Mild to Moderate)

* **Dosage:** **1.5 to 3 grams** of pure L-citrulline daily.
* **Protocol:** Often taken as 750 mg twice daily or 1.5 grams once daily.
* **Duration:** Improvements are generally observed after **1 month** of
  consistent use.

### Pure L-Citrulline vs. Citrulline Malate Conversion

It is vital to distinguish between the two forms to avoid under-dosing. 
**Citrulline Malate** is bonded with malic acid, diluting the actual citrulline
content.

| Target Actual Citrulline | Pure L-Citrulline | Citrulline Malate (2:1 Ratio) | Citrulline Malate (1:1 Ratio) |
|:-------------------------|:------------------|:------------------------------|:------------------------------|
| **3 grams**              | 3 g               | 4.5 g                         | 6 g                           |
| **6 grams**              | 6 g               | 9 g                           | 12 g                          |
| **8 grams**              | 8 g               | 12 g                          | 16 g                          |

*Most research supporting athletic performance uses the **2:1 ratio** or pure
citrulline. Be wary of supplements using a 1:1 ratio without adjusting the total
scoop size.*

## Safety and Side Effects

L-citrulline is generally well-tolerated with a high safety profile.

* **Safe Upper Limit:** Clinical studies have safely used acute doses up to **15
  grams** and daily doses up to **10 grams** without serious adverse events.
* **Side Effects:**  Doses exceeding 10 grams may increase the risk of
  gastrointestinal distress (bloating, diarrhea), particularly with the malate
  form due to its acidity.
* **Timing:** Unlike caffeine, L-citrulline does not cause jitters, but taking
  very large single doses (>10g) on an empty stomach may cause discomfort in
  sensitive individuals.
* **Contraindications:** Individuals taking medications for blood pressure or
  erectile dysfunction (nitrates/PDE5 inhibitors) should consult a healthcare
  provider, as the combined vasodilatory effects could cause blood pressure to
  drop too low.
* **Comparison to L-Arginine:** L-citrulline is often preferred over L-arginine
  because it avoids the gastrointestinal issues frequently associated with
  high-dose arginine supplementation and provides more stable increases in
  plasma arginine levels.

## Interactions with L-Citrulline

L-citrulline interacts synergistically with several common supplements,
primarily enhancing **nitric oxide production**, **energy metabolism**, and 
**blood flow**. However, it requires caution when combined with certain
cardiovascular medications.

### Performance Stacks (Synergistic Benefits)

Combining L-citrulline with other ergogenic aids often yields greater results
than using them in isolation due to complementary mechanisms of action.

* **Caffeine:** This is a **high-synergy combination** (rated 4/5). While
  caffeine stimulates the central nervous system for focus and power,
  L-citrulline ensures adequate blood flow and oxygen delivery to muscles.
  Crucially, L-citrulline may help **mitigate caffeine-induced jitters** or
  blood pressure spikes by promoting vasodilation, creating a balanced "smooth
  energy" effect without compromising the stimulant's efficacy.
* **Creatine:** These two address different energy systems. Creatine replenishes
  **ATP** for explosive power, while L-citrulline improves nutrient delivery and
  waste removal (ammonia/lactate). Together, they support both **strength output
  ** and **endurance capacity**, potentially optimizing creatine absorption
  through improved circulation.
* **Beta-Alanine:** This duo targets fatigue from two angles. Beta-alanine
  buffers muscle acidity (the "burn") via carnosine production, while
  L-citrulline enhances oxygen delivery and ammonia clearance. This combination
  is highly effective for **high-intensity interval training (HIIT)** and
  resistance training, allowing for higher volume and delayed fatigue.
* **L-Arginine:** Although they share similar goals, taking them together is
  **more effective** than taking either alone. L-arginine provides an immediate
  spike in plasma arginine levels, while L-citrulline sustains these levels over
  a longer period by bypassing liver metabolism and inhibiting the enzyme
  (arginase) that breaks down arginine. This creates a rapid yet prolonged
  nitric oxide boost.

### Medicine Interactions (Contraindications)

While safe with most nutrients, L-citrulline's potent vasodilatory effects pose
risks when combined with specific compounds that also lower blood pressure.

* **Blood Pressure Medications:** Combining L-citrulline with
  antihypertensives (e.g., ACE inhibitors, calcium channel blockers) can cause
  an **additive effect**, potentially dropping blood pressure too low
  (hypotension). Symptoms may include dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting.
  Monitoring is essential if combining these.
* **Nitrates and PDE5 Inhibitors:** Do **not** combine L-citrulline with
  prescription nitrates (for chest pain) or erectile dysfunction drugs like
  sildenafil (Viagra) or tadalafil (Cialis). The combined vasodilation can lead
  to a **dangerous, precipitous drop in blood pressure**.
* **Other Vasodilators:** Caution is advised when stacking with other nitric
  oxide boosters (e.g., high-dose beetroot extract) or herbs like Hawthorn, as
  the cumulative effect may exceed intended limits.

## Storage

To preserve the potency and stability of L-citrulline, strict control over
environmental factors is essential. The amino acid is chemically stable under
proper conditions but degrades rapidly when exposed to specific stressors.

### Critical Environmental Factors

* **Moisture Control (Most Critical):** L-citrulline, particularly the 
  **citrulline malate** form, is **hygroscopic**, meaning it actively absorbs
  water from the air. Exposure to humidity causes clumping, reduces solubility,
  and accelerates chemical degradation. Containers must be kept **tightly
  sealed** immediately after use.
* **Temperature:** Store in a **cool environment**, ideally between **15°C and
  25°C (59°F to 77°F)**. High temperatures accelerate decomposition; 
  significant potency loss can occur within weeks if exposed to heat sources
  (e.g., near stoves, in hot cars, or direct sunlight).
* **Light Exposure:** Protect from **direct sunlight and UV rays**, which can
  break down the molecular structure over time. Opaque containers or dark
  storage locations (like a cupboard) are preferred over clear jars on
  countertops.

## Shelf Life and Stability

* **Powder Form:** When stored correctly in a cool, dry, and dark place,
  L-citrulline powder typically maintains full potency for **24 to 36 months**.
* **Liquid Form:** L-citrulline is **unstable in liquid solutions** over time
  due to hydrolysis and potential microbial growth. It should be mixed with
  water or juice **immediately before consumption** and not stored as a
  pre-mixed drink.
* **Signs of Degradation:** Discard the supplement if you notice significant *
  *color changes** (yellowing), unusual **odors**, or if the powder has hardened
  into a solid block that cannot be broken apart, indicating severe moisture
  exposure.

## Best Practices  

| Factor        | Recommendation                     | Reason                                               |
|:--------------|:-----------------------------------|:-----------------------------------------------------|
| **Location**  | Cool, dry cupboard or pantry       | Avoids heat spikes and humidity (e.g., bathrooms).   |
| **Container** | Airtight, opaque (or kept in dark) | Prevents moisture absorption and UV degradation.     |
| **Handling**  | Use dry scoops only                | Introducing wet spoons introduces moisture/mold.     |
| **Form**      | Powder preferred over liquid       | Powder is stable for years; liquid degrades quickly. |

**Note:** Do not store L-citrulline in the refrigerator unless the environment
is extremely humid, as the temperature fluctuation when removing the container
can cause condensation inside, introducing moisture. A consistent,
room-temperature dark cabinet is generally superior.
