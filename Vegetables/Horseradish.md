# Horseradish

Horseradish is a **root vegetable**. It is the hardy, perennial root of the
*Armoracia rusticana* plant, which belongs to the *Brassicaceae*v family (also
known as the mustard or cabbage family). This makes it closely related to other
root vegetables and cruciferous vegetables like radishes, mustard, wasabi,
broccoli, and cabbage. It is primarily cultivated and consumed as a pungent
**condiment** or spice due to its intense heat, botanically and culinarily, 
it is classified as a vegetable root.

## Key Nutrients

**Vitamin C** is the standout nutrient in horseradish. A 100-gram serving
provides approximately **25 mg**, which is about **28–41%** of the daily
recommended value. This powerful antioxidant supports immune function, collagen
production, and protects cells from free radical damage.

It also contains moderate amounts of:

* **Folate (Vitamin B9):** Essential for cell division and DNA synthesis (~14%
  DV).
* **Dietary Fiber:** Aids digestion and gut health (~3.3g per 100g).
* **Minerals:** Including **potassium** (muscle/nerve function), **calcium**
  (bone health), **magnesium**, **zinc** (immune support), and **manganese**.

## Health Benefits

The root is rich in bioactive compounds called **glucosinolates** (specifically
**sinigrin**) and **isothiocyanates**. When the root is grated, these compounds
are released and are responsible for both its pungent heat and its medicinal
properties.

* **Antimicrobial & Immune Support:** Its compounds have antibacterial and
  antiviral properties, historically used to fight respiratory infections and
  clear sinus congestion.
* **Anti-Inflammatory & Antioxidant:** Helps reduce chronic inflammation and
  oxidative stress, potentially lowering the risk of chronic diseases.
* **Cancer-Fighting Potential:** Glucosinolates have been studied for their
  ability to inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells (particularly in the
  colon, lung, and stomach) and prevent tumor formation.
* **Digestive Aid:** Stimulates the production of digestive enzymes and bile,
  which can improve nutrient absorption and reduce bloating.
* **Low-Calorie:** With only about **48 calories per 100g**, it adds intense
  flavor without significant caloric load.
